## How It Works

```
User: "Send an email to Sarah about tomorrow's meeting"
        ↓
Orceum AI identifies the right app + action + parameters
        ↓
Execution Engine resolves the user's installation → decrypts credentials → calls your app
        ↓
Your app executes the action and returns the result
        ↓
AI reports back to the user
```

Orceum handles everything between the user's intent and your app's action:
- **Intent parsing** — the AI extracts the right action and parameters from natural language
- **Credential management** — tokens and API keys are encrypted at rest and injected at call time
- **Error recovery** — 401s trigger token refreshes, 429s trigger retries
- **Event routing** — your app can push events back; Orceum's AI decides what to do with them

## The Developer Contract

As an app developer, you need to:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define your manifest">
    Describe what your app can do — actions, parameters, and descriptions the AI will read.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register your app">
    Call the Orceum API once to register your endpoint, auth config, and manifest.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Handle action calls">
    Receive `POST` requests from Orceum with `{ event, event_data, timestamp }` and return a JSON result.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optionally push events">
    When something happens in your service (new email, calendar update), push it to Orceum. The AI takes it from there.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## App Types at a Glance

| Type | Transport | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Native** | HTTPS POST | Any HTTP API you control |
| **MCP** | MCP Protocol (SSE / Streamable HTTP) | Tool servers implementing the Model Context Protocol |

<Note>
System and Skill app types are internal to Orceum and not available for third-party developers.
</Note>
